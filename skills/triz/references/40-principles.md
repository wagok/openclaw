# 40 Inventive Principles of TRIZ

## 1. Segmentation
Divide an object into independent parts. Make it sectional or modular. Increase degree of fragmentation.
- Modular furniture, sectional sofa
- Replace mainframe with distributed microservices

## 2. Taking out (Extraction)
Separate an interfering part or property. Extract only the necessary part.
- Noise-canceling: extract and invert the noise signal
- Remote work: extract the worker from the office

## 3. Local quality
Change uniform structure to non-uniform. Different parts perform different functions. Place each part in conditions most favorable for its operation.
- Bifocal lenses (different zones for different distances)
- Microservice with different scaling per component

## 4. Asymmetry
Replace symmetrical form with asymmetrical. If already asymmetrical, increase asymmetry.
- Asymmetric tire tread for better grip
- Ergonomic keyboard

## 5. Merging
Combine identical or similar objects/operations in space or time. Make operations parallel.
- Multi-tool, Swiss army knife
- Batch processing, bulk API calls

## 6. Universality
Make a part perform multiple functions, eliminating the need for other parts.
- Smartphone replaces camera, GPS, calculator, music player
- Sofa-bed

## 7. "Nested doll" (Matryoshka)
Place one object inside another. Pass one through a cavity in another.
- Telescoping antenna
- Nested containers, Docker layers

## 8. Anti-weight (Counterweight)
Compensate weight by merging with another object that provides lift. Compensate by interaction with environment.
- Helium balloon on heavy sign
- Hydrofoil lifts hull out of water

## 9. Preliminary anti-action
If a harmful action must occur, perform counter-action in advance. Pre-stress to counter operating stresses.
- Pre-stressed concrete
- Vaccination (preliminary counter-action against disease)

## 10. Preliminary action
Perform required changes in advance (fully or partially). Pre-arrange objects for most convenient position.
- Pre-pasted wallpaper
- Staging environment before production deploy

## 11. Beforehand cushioning
Prepare emergency means beforehand to compensate for low reliability.
- Airbags, backup systems
- Circuit breaker pattern in software

## 12. Equipotentiality
Change conditions so object need not be raised or lowered.
- Canal locks (bring water to the boat, not boat to the water)
- Level loading docks

## 13. The other way round
Invert the action. Make the movable part fixed and the fixed part movable. Turn the object upside down.
- Treadmill (person stays, ground moves)
- Push vs pull deployment

## 14. Spheroidality — Curvature
Replace linear parts with curved. Use rollers, balls, spirals. Replace linear motion with rotary.
- Ball bearings instead of sliding surfaces
- Scroll compressor

## 15. Dynamics
Allow characteristics to change to be optimal at each stage. Divide into parts capable of movement relative to each other. Make rigid things flexible or adaptive.
- Adjustable steering wheel
- Dynamic pricing, auto-scaling infrastructure

## 16. Partial or excessive action
If 100% is hard to achieve, use slightly less or slightly more. Overshoot then trim.
- Overfill a mold, then trim excess
- Over-provision cloud resources, scale down later

## 17. Another dimension
Move to 2D or 3D. Use multi-story arrangement. Tilt or reorient the object. Use the other side.
- Multi-level parking
- 3D chip stacking

## 18. Mechanical vibration
Use oscillation. Increase frequency to ultrasonic. Use resonance. Use piezoelectric vibrators.
- Ultrasonic cleaning
- Vibration-assisted drilling

## 19. Periodic action
Replace continuous action with periodic or pulsed. If already periodic, change frequency. Use pauses for other actions.
- Pulsed laser vs continuous
- Batch processing with sleep intervals

## 20. Continuity of useful action
Carry on work continuously. Eliminate idle and intermediate motions. Use full capacity.
- Continuous casting of steel
- Streaming processing vs batch

## 21. Skipping (Rushing through)
Conduct a process or certain stages at high speed.
- Flash pasteurization
- Quick-setting cement

## 22. "Blessing in disguise" (Convert harm to benefit)
Use harmful factors to achieve a positive effect. Eliminate harm by combining with another harm. Amplify harm to the point it ceases to be harmful.
- Use waste heat for heating
- Controlled forest fires prevent larger fires

## 23. Feedback
Introduce feedback to improve a process. If feedback exists, change its magnitude or influence.
- Thermostat
- A/B testing, monitoring alerts

## 24. Intermediary (Mediator)
Use an intermediate carrier or process. Temporarily merge one object with another that can be easily removed.
- Catalyst in chemical reaction
- API gateway, message broker

## 25. Self-service
Make object service itself. Use waste resources, energy, or substances.
- Self-cleaning oven
- Auto-healing systems, self-documenting code

## 26. Copying
Replace expensive, fragile, or inconvenient object with simpler/cheaper copy. Replace with optical copies (images). Use IR or UV copies.
- Virtual reality training instead of real equipment
- Digital twin

## 27. Cheap short-living objects
Replace expensive object with collection of cheap ones, sacrificing certain qualities (e.g., longevity).
- Disposable medical instruments
- Spot instances in cloud computing

## 28. Mechanics substitution
Replace mechanical system with optical, acoustic, thermal, or electromagnetic. Use electric, magnetic, and electromagnetic fields. Replace fields (static → moving, fixed → changing, random → structured).
- Magnetic levitation instead of wheels
- Optical fiber instead of copper wire

## 29. Pneumatics and hydraulics
Use gas and liquid parts instead of solid. Inflatable, filled with liquid, air cushion, hydrostatic, hydro-reactive.
- Air cushion packaging
- Hydraulic press

## 30. Flexible shells and thin films
Replace traditional constructions with flexible shells and thin films. Isolate from external environment using flexible shells.
- Shrink wrap
- Bubble wrap, inflatable structures

## 31. Porous materials
Make object porous or add porous elements. If already porous, use the pores (fill with substance).
- Sponge, foam
- Porous catalysts (increased surface area)

## 32. Color changes
Change color of object or environment. Change transparency. Use colored additives to observe processes.
- Thermochromic paint (changes color with temperature)
- Syntax highlighting in code

## 33. Homogeneity
Make objects interact with a given object of the same material (or close to it).
- Ice container for ice cream (both are ice)
- Diamond cutting diamond

## 34. Discarding and recovering
Make portions of object that have fulfilled their function disappear or modify them during operation. Restore consumable parts during operation.
- Dissolving surgical sutures
- Garbage collection in programming languages

## 35. Parameter changes
Change aggregate state, concentration, flexibility, temperature, pressure, volume, or other physical parameters.
- Freeze food for transport, thaw for use
- Compress data for transfer, decompress for use

## 36. Phase transitions
Use phenomena occurring during phase transitions (volume changes, heat release/absorption).
- Heat pipes use evaporation/condensation cycle
- Phase-change cooling in electronics

## 37. Thermal expansion
Use thermal expansion or contraction. Use multiple materials with different thermal expansion coefficients.
- Bimetallic strip in thermostat
- Shrink-fitting (heat outer part, cool inner, assemble, equalize)

## 38. Strong oxidants
Replace common air with enriched air or oxygen. Replace enriched air with pure oxygen. Use ionized oxygen. Use ozone.
- Oxygen enrichment in steel production
- Ozone for water purification

## 39. Inert atmosphere
Replace normal environment with inert. Add neutral parts or inert additives. Carry out process in vacuum.
- Argon welding
- Vacuum packaging

## 40. Composite materials
Replace homogeneous materials with composite. Use materials with complementary properties.
- Carbon fiber (strong + lightweight)
- Reinforced concrete (compression + tension)
